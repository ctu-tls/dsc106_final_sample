A deeper look into temperature trends across the United States

What are we comparing?

Climate change is shaped by more than one factor. In this project, we compare surface temperature, aerosol concentration, incoming solar radiation, and carbon dioxide to explore how different atmospheric variables relate to temperature change.

tas Surface Air Temperature

This measures air temperature near Earth's surface. It is our main outcome variable: when tas rises, it means the surface climate is getting warmer.

od550aer Aerosol Optical Depth

This measures how much tiny airborne particles block or scatter light. More aerosols can reduce incoming sunlight and create short-term cooling or disruption.

rsdt Incoming Solar Radiation

This measures solar energy reaching the top of the atmosphere. More incoming solar radiation can add energy to the climate system and support warming.

co2 Carbon Dioxide

CO₂ traps heat in the atmosphere. Unlike aerosols, its warming effect is more persistent, making it an important driver of long-term temperature change.

The key question is not whether one variable explains everything. Instead, we ask: how much do these factors appear to contribute to temperature change, and where does the long-term warming signal remain visible?

What Explains Temperature Change?

Select a U.S. state and time window from 1960 to 2014. This view estimates how changes in CO₂, aerosols, and incoming solar radiation are associated with changes in surface temperature.

We use a regression model to estimate each factor’s contribution. These values show modeled associations, not direct causal proof.

tas Surface Air Temperature

This is the temperature outcome we are trying to explain. Observed ΔT shows how much surface temperature changed during the selected time window.

CO₂ Carbon Dioxide

CO₂ traps heat in the atmosphere. In the model, a positive CO₂ contribution means rising CO₂ is associated with warming.

od550aer Aerosols

Aerosols are tiny particles that can scatter or block sunlight. Their contribution may appear as cooling or warming depending on the selected state and period.

rsdt Incoming Solar Radiation

rsdt measures solar energy reaching the top of the atmosphere. It helps represent how incoming energy relates to temperature change.
